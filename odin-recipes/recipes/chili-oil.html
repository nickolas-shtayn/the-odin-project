Chili Oil

Crank up the flavor of any dish with this homemade chili oil! Forget the bland, store-bought stuff. This recipe creates a vibrant condiment bursting with fiery heat, aromatic spices, and a touch of tang.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons or 10 grams of coriander seeds
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 5 star anise
  • 3 black cardomom pods
  • 1/3 cup of Sichuan peppercorns
  • 4 large cloves of garlic, lightly bruised and left in their skin
  • 1 quart of neutral tasting oil (canola, peanut, or sesame oil)
  • 1 cup of Sichuan chili flakes
  • 1.5 teaspoons of fine sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon of black vinegar

Steps

  1. place all the spices (coriander seeds, cinnamon stick, star anise, black cardamom pods, and Sichuan peppercorns) and garlic cloves in a medium sauce pot.
  2. Cover the spices with neutral tasting oil and heat over low heat to 225-250 degrees Fahrenheit (107-120 degrees Celsius). Let the spices infuse the oil for 1-2 hours, or until fragrant. If the garlic starts to get golden brown, remove it from the oil.
  3. In a large bowl, mix together the Sichuan chili flakes, sea salt, and black vinegar.
  4. Once the oil is fragrant, turn the heat up to medium-high and heat the oil to 300 degrees Fahrenheit (148 degrees Celsius).
  5. Strain the hot oil through a fine mesh strainer into the bowl with the chili flakes, salt, and vinegar.
  6. Let the chili oil mixture sit at room temperature until cool.
  7. Pour the chili oil into mason jars or other airtight containers.
